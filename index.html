A Brief History

High School at Louis E. Dieruff

2014 - 2018

As a student who played sports, had many friends, and was smart, I can only remember those times as not giving it all I had. Between distraction, lack of purpose, and no path to take, I felt as if I were a small fish in a vast ocean with nowhere to swim. Freshman, sophomore, and junior years are a blur that as I remember felt more like an insignificant blip and not memories I choose to cherish. My final year is when I began to care about my future. These were the inital signs of me becoming someone who wanted to forge his own path.

Dodd Hiram W Elementary School Scholer"

2018

Senior year, I was starting my college search and looking for opportunities. I had gone to this elementary so naturally they entered my name to the scholarship raffle. Raffle winners, which consisted of myself and three other seniors were to speak in the 5th grade class rooms.Then based of popularity the kids would vote on the winner of the $500 dollar scholarship. My experience speaking with those kids was profoundly and deeply impactful. In each class I found bright smiles and mountains of hope that could endure any storm or climb any ocean crest. I realized that our age gap was only a formality of time and we were all the same internally. Only difference was I was leaving that stage of life and they were coming into it. Seeing them was a force that convinced me to have that kind of hope in myself and what I could do. Winning the major vote of those kids means the world to me because, as I had seen them, they also had seen me.

Bloomsburg University of Pennsylvania"

2018 - 2022

Where I turned opportunity into desired outcomes. Summer 2018 the school offered an acceptance program where underprivileged students could get their feet wet and get a taste of the university pace. That summer something clicked, I wanted to and did thrive. Taking hold of my own path I put in time, effort, and passion into my work, time management and my success. Many moments in university changed the philosophy that I used to define my life, and most importantly transformed the self expression of work ethic. I recognized the importance of a positive outcome and knew that the time would be well spent if I continuously engaged every single aspect of the era. This idea is still at the core of how I think when leaving or entering an interaction. In my four years of attendance at the university I understood that learning or gathering information, for educated and informed decisions should not stop. Continuously learning and reaching new heights is a fundamental pillar of success. For this reason in my own story I will be continuously growing my skills, education, and higher learning.
